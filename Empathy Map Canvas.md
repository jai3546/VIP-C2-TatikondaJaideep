## Empathy Map Canvas

An empathy map is a simple and effective tool used to understand users by capturing their behaviors, thoughts, feelings, goals, and challenges. It helps the development team design solutions that truly address user needs and improve the overall shopping experience.

By empathizing with customers and sellers, ShopVerse aims to build an intuitive, secure, and efficient e-commerce platform.

# Empathy Map 1: Online Shopper

## User Persona

| Attribute | Details |
|:---|:---|
| **Name** | Arjun Reddy |
| **Age** | 24 Years |
| **Occupation** | Software Engineer |
| **Location** | Hyderabad, India |
| **Tech Proficiency** | Moderate to High |

### SAYS

- "I want to find products quickly without wasting time."
- "I prefer shopping websites with simple navigation."
- "Secure payments are very important to me."
- "I like tracking my orders from one place."
- "Product reviews help me make better decisions."

### THINKS

- "Can I trust this website with my payment details?"
- "Will the product look exactly like the images?"
- "Is the delivery fast and reliable?"
- "Can I return or cancel the order if required?"
- "A clean website usually gives me more confidence."

### DOES

- Searches for products using keywords.
- Filters products based on category and price.
- Adds multiple products to the shopping cart.
- Compares prices before placing an order.
- Reviews product ratings and customer feedback.
- Tracks previous and current orders.

### FEELS

- **Frustrated** when products are difficult to find.
- **Concerned** about payment security.
- **Satisfied** when checkout is simple and transparent.
- **Confident** when order tracking is available.
- **Happy** with a responsive and user-friendly interface.

### Pain Points

1. Poor search and filtering options.
2. Complicated checkout process.
3. Concerns about payment security.
4. Limited order tracking.
5. Slow and confusing website navigation.

### Goals

1. Find products quickly.
2. Complete secure online payments.
3. Track orders easily.
4. Discover products through organized categories.
5. Enjoy a smooth shopping experience.

# Empathy Map 2: Seller / Store Administrator

## User Persona

| Attribute | Details |
|:---|:---|
| **Name** | Neha Verma |
| **Age** | 31 Years |
| **Occupation** | Small Business Owner |
| **Location** | Bengaluru, India |
| **Tech Proficiency** | Moderate |

### SAYS

- "I need one dashboard to manage everything."
- "Adding products should be quick and simple."
- "I want to monitor orders in real time."
- "Sales reports help me understand my business."
- "Managing customers should not be complicated."

### THINKS

- "Is my store data secure?"
- "How can I improve my sales performance?"
- "Managing inventory should be effortless."
- "The dashboard should be simple to use."
- "Only authorized users should access admin features."

### DOES

- Logs into the admin dashboard daily.
- Adds, edits, and removes products.
- Manages categories and product inventory.
- Reviews customer orders and updates their status.
- Checks sales reports and customer statistics.

### FEELS

- **Overwhelmed** by complex admin panels.
- **Productive** when everything is managed from one dashboard.
- **Confident** when business reports are available.
- **Satisfied** with quick product management tools.
- **Concerned** about unauthorized access.

### Pain Points

1. Managing products across multiple tools.
2. Difficulty monitoring inventory.
3. Limited sales insights.
4. Time-consuming order management.
5. Weak access control for admin operations.

### Goals

1. Manage products efficiently.
2. Monitor orders in one place.
3. Track business performance.
4. Maintain secure admin access.
5. Grow the business with better insights.
